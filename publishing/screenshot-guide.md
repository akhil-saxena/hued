# Play Store Screenshots Guide

Generate these 5-8 screenshots at 1080x1920 (9:16 portrait).
Use the actual app on a device with real photo data for authentic palettes.

## Screenshot 1: Hero
**What to capture:** Main screen with current week palette, color names expanded, poetic description visible
**Text overlay:** "your life in color"
**Why:** First impression. Shows the core value immediately.

## Screenshot 2: Time Periods
**What to capture:** Month view showing a rich palette, then swipe to year view
**Text overlay:** "week. month. year."
**Why:** Shows the time-switching feature.

## Screenshot 3: Color Names
**What to capture:** Expanded hero card showing top 3 color swatches + full 5-color list with names
**Text overlay:** "31,000 color names"
**Why:** The naming feature is a differentiator.

## Screenshot 4: Share Card
**What to capture:** Share overlay showing the rendered 9:16 card with save/share/close pills
**Text overlay:** "share your palette"
**Why:** Shareability is a core feature.

## Screenshot 5: History
**What to capture:** Scrolled down to earlier section showing multiple historical palette strips
**Text overlay:** "scroll through your color history"
**Why:** Shows depth and the chronological story.

## Screenshot 6: Settings
**What to capture:** Settings page showing palette depth slider, weighted bands, folder toggles
**Text overlay:** "your colors, your way"
**Why:** Shows customization without complexity.

## Screenshot 7: Processing
**What to capture:** Initial processing screen showing "247 of 789 images"
**Text overlay:** "all processing on-device"
**Why:** Privacy messaging.

## Screenshot 8: Widget
**What to capture:** Home screen with hued widget showing weekly palette strip
**Text overlay:** "always on your home screen"
**Why:** Widget is a retention feature.
